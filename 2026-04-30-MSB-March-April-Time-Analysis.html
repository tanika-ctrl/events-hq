Highlights

RD — A Month-Long Production Marathon
RD worked on MSB for 21 days across March, logging 137.5 hours — an average of 6.5 hours per working day on a single client. The engagement moved through three distinct phases: research and cataloguing (5–15 March), video production of operational procedures (16–22 March), then advanced multi-video production at Cat A rates (24–31 March). RD is not visible in April, suggesting the primary production body of work was delivered by end of March.
Tier Shift — Cat B to Cat A on 24 March
RD's billing tier shifted from Cat B Creative to Cat A Expert Work on 24 March, aligning with the transition from cataloguing and early editing into delivering fully produced, complex video assets (manifold, TSi 150CC, TUG, safety systems). This is correctly applied — the later-phase work required higher technical production skill and was more commercially valuable output.
CJ — Supporting in March, Leading in April
CJ joined from 23 March on training videos (7h across 3 sessions) while RD was still active. By April, CJ holds the engagement alone — 15 hours across 6 sessions, all Cat A. The structure of April (4 entries in 8–10 April, then a 12-day gap, then a final 4.5h session) is consistent with a submit-and-review cycle. CJ is likely handling final revisions and sign-off.
RK — Direction at the Right Moment
RK's single 6-hour admin and direction entry on 19 March lands exactly at the pivot point between cataloguing and production. This wasn't random — 19 March was mid-way through the control panel edits and shortly before the tier shift. RK almost certainly provided the creative/commercial direction that set up the Cat A production push in the final week of March.
Volume vs. Client Scope
165.5 hours across two months is a significant engagement. The project covers a wide range of training video subjects: control panel, grease points, fluids check, manual override valves, TSi 150CC, TUG, water manifold, mast raising, crew safety, shutdown procedures, and walk-around. This is a comprehensive training library — not a single video.

Analysis

MSB was the dominant external production engagement for March. At 150.5 hours logged across three team members, it absorbed the majority of Subsurface's creative capacity for the month — with RD essentially working full-time on MSB from the 5th to the 31st. This is a meaningful engagement at scale.

The project's shape is clear in the data: a structured, phased approach from discovery through to delivery. RD's methodical cataloguing phase (11–15 March, 33 hours) is notable — five consecutive 5–8 hour days watching source footage and building a content map. This kind of upfront structure investment often pays back in production efficiency, and the subsequent pace of the production phase (8-hour editing days, multiple videos completed) suggests it did.

The Cat B to Cat A billing shift on 24 March is correctly timed. The early work — planning, cataloguing, research-based editing — is genuine Cat B creative labour. The final production phase — technically complex multi-source video construction for a specialist drilling equipment client — warrants Cat A. If there are any invoicing queries on tier, the granular descriptions in the timesheet make a robust case.

April tells the completion story. RD's exit from the engagement is abrupt — no wind-down sessions, no overlap handover entries visible. CJ's April sessions read like CJ already knew the content well from the March training video work, picking up where she left off without needing significant onboarding. The 8–10 April cluster (7 hours in three days) followed by silence and then a further 4.5h session is a textbook client review-and-revise pattern.

The outstanding question is whether MSB is now closed or ongoing. If the April entries represent final delivery, this engagement has concluded and should be marked as such. If more work is expected, it's worth agreeing scope and timeline now — CJ's April capacity is being shared with DBR's operational build-out, and a third wave of MSB work would need to be planned against that constraint.
